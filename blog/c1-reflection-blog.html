  1. What's your take on the DBC/EDA experience?

    2. I feel as if all the learning is up to you, you have to seek the information rather than be spoonfeed like traditional education. Dev Academy is a community, rather than students trying to be better than other students. I thought the kitchen analogy that Shereef used was effective in explaining the best way to learn. DBC/EDA seem to facilitate a culture where people aren't afraid to ask for help and are keen to help others.

  2. What are your impressions?

    3. I feel as if it is going to be incredibly fast paced and challenging. It is a slight shock to the system not having as much guidance as I have had at school and university, but feel as if I am achieving more and learning more effectively. Going forward I am excited and curious as to what is to come!

  3. How do you see yourself engaging with this type of culture

    4. I see myself doing well in this culture. This week I have felt like other people have shot ahead in a sense, but I want to be able to help others if I know any skills/info that they don't. I'm definitely a team player. My perception is that Dev supplies a range of resources that are relevant to what you are doing but do not tell you exactly how to do it. This week it made me feel as if I was incapable, but I understand that it is simply done this well because it is the most effective way in helping me learn.

  4. Have your expectations of EDA changed? If so, how?

    5. Yes they have definitely changed. I knew EDA would be challenging but I didn't realise how challenging. Now I know why "time management" has been brought up so much already.

  5. Are you excited to participate in this kind of learning environment? Does it make you nervous?

    6. Yes I am excited. I feel like there is a lot of unknown and yes this makes me feel nervouce, but excited, as I have learned so much just in this first week.
